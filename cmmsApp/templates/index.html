Electrical & Instrument Junction Boxes for Safe Field Connections

A junction box is an enclosure used to safely terminate and distribute power, control or instrumentation wiring at cable junction points. It provides protection, organisation and easier troubleshooting for field circuits.

Use a junction box when you need to extend wiring, add connection points, or route multiple conduits into one location, while keeping terminations protected and compliant.

How we work

Junction Box Delivery Workflow

We follow a structured process to make sure your junction boxes arrive site-ready with the correct layout, terminations and protection level.

Share your wiring philosophy, I O list, cable schedule, gland requirements and installation location. We will align the enclosure design to your environment and maintenance needs.

  1. 1

    Requirements and Environment

    Confirm indoor or outdoor location, cable entry direction, number of cores, terminals, labels, and IP rating expectations.

  2. 2

    Enclosure Design and Layout

    Select material (SS316 or mild steel), size, gland plate arrangement, terminal blocks and internal wiring layout.

  3. 3

    Manufacture and Assembly

    Fabrication, finishing, drilling and fitment of hardware including hinges, latches, bolts and accessories. Assembly of terminals and identification as required.

  4. 4

    QA and Supply Coordination

    Basic checks for enclosure sealing, fit, labeling and delivery readiness, followed by packing and dispatch to site.

Installation notes

Where Junction Boxes Are Typically Located

Common placement depends on duct runs, instrument field distribution and accessibility for maintenance.

Duct and Conduit Intersections

Installed at intersections of duct runs to provide a safe termination and routing point.

Across Process Facilities

Used to connect multiple field instruments to control systems and distributed panels.

Aligned to Baselines and Routing

Locations are coordinated along routing baselines so duct patterns remain consistent and maintainable.

Outdoor Signal Wiring Points

Outdoor instrument signal wiring JBs should be fully installed with mounting, hardware, nameplates, terminal blocks and wiring.

Maintenance Access Areas

Placed where technicians can isolate, test and repair circuits without disturbing upstream cables.

Indoor and Outdoor Use

Junction boxes are used both indoors and outdoors as connection points for power, control and instrumentation.
